Computers evolved from massive room-sized machines like ENIAC in the 1940s that performed basic calculations to smaller, more powerful devices. The 1970s-80s brought personal computers that made computing accessible to individuals and businesses. The internet revolution of the 1990s-2000s connected these machines globally and enabled web-based applications. Today's computers have become ubiquitous—from smartphones to quantum computers—offering unprecedented processing power and connectivity that transforms nearly every aspect of modern life.
